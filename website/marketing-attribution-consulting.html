When marketing attribution consulting becomes necessary

The problem is rarely a missing chart. It is usually a disagreement about identity, funnel logic, source data, or what “influenced” actually means.

  • Marketing, sales, and finance report different pipeline numbers.
  • Platform-reported conversions cannot be reconciled to closed revenue.
  • First-touch and last-touch reports lead to opposite budget decisions.
  • Campaign naming, UTMs, and CRM source fields are inconsistent.
  • Multiple contacts contribute to one B2B opportunity.
  • Offline referrals, events, and partner touches disappear from reporting.
  • Only one person understands the attribution spreadsheet or tool.
  • Leadership cannot explain why a channel received credit.

An important limitation

Attribution measures evidence. It does not prove perfect causality.

A credible attribution system makes assumptions visible and supports better comparisons. It cannot know with certainty which single touch “caused” every sale.

  • We separate sourced, influenced, and self-reported attribution where useful.
  • We validate outputs against known opportunities and business context.
  • We recommend experiments or incrementality analysis when attribution alone cannot answer the causal question.
  • We do not manufacture precision the underlying data cannot support.

Marketing attribution consulting FAQs

Do we need a data warehouse first?

No. We recommend the smallest reliable architecture. Some engagements use an existing warehouse; others begin with CRM exports or a governed reporting process.

Which attribution model is best?

There is no universally best model. The right approach depends on your sales cycle, available identity data, offline touches, decision being made, and how much complexity your team can maintain.

Can you work with HubSpot or Salesforce?

Yes. We can work with CRM data alongside advertising, web analytics, product, billing, and warehouse data. The specific sources are agreed during the Blueprint.

Do you manage campaigns after building attribution?

No. We build the measurement infrastructure and reporting. Your team or agency continues to manage media, creative, SEO, and campaign optimization.

What if our tracking is incomplete?

That is common. The Blueprint identifies what can be answered now, which gaps materially affect decisions, and the collection improvements worth prioritizing.
