# Revenue Data Blueprint — Delivery Checklist

Internal operating template. Both founders should review and approve these defaults before using them in a client statement of work. This is not legal advice.

## Recommended default commercial boundary

- One primary business decision or reporting question
- Up to five relevant source systems
- Up to three existing reports or dashboards reviewed in depth
- One 45-minute kickoff
- Up to two 30-minute stakeholder interviews
- One 60-minute findings presentation
- One consolidated clarification round after presentation
- Five-business-day delivery clock starts only after kickoff and required access are complete
- Historical-data repair, production implementation, and ongoing report operation are excluded

Any exception should be written into the statement of work and reflected in the price before kickoff.

## Before accepting payment

- [ ] Confirm the primary decision in one sentence.
- [ ] Identify the executive or operational owner of that decision.
- [ ] List the systems and reports expected to be in scope.
- [ ] Confirm that system owners can provide the required access.
- [ ] Confirm the client can attend kickoff and findings sessions.
- [ ] Identify known compliance or data-residency constraints.
- [ ] Agree on the legal entity, billing contact, payment schedule, and currency.
- [ ] Execute the statement of work and any required NDA/DPA.

## Kickoff completion criteria

- [ ] Primary question and success criteria approved
- [ ] Stakeholders and system owners identified
- [ ] Source and report list confirmed
- [ ] Minimum access received and tested
- [ ] Known exclusions documented
- [ ] Findings-session date scheduled
- [ ] Five-business-day delivery start date recorded

## Required deliverables

- [ ] Executive summary and decision statement
- [ ] Stakeholder/business-question map
- [ ] Source, metric, and reporting inventory
- [ ] Data-quality and pipeline risk assessment
- [ ] Attribution/reporting gap analysis where relevant
- [ ] Recommended target architecture or lightweight process
- [ ] Prioritized 30/60/90-day roadmap, or shorter roadmap where appropriate
- [ ] Implementation boundaries and assumptions
- [ ] Recorded findings presentation
- [ ] Fixed-price implementation proposal when Prime is a fit

## Quality review before presentation

- [ ] Every finding cites the source, report, interview, or test supporting it.
- [ ] Facts, assumptions, and recommendations are clearly distinguished.
- [ ] Metric definitions are written in business language.
- [ ] The recommendation is the smallest reliable solution.
- [ ] Known data limitations are explicit.
- [ ] No unsupported performance or ROI outcome is promised.
- [ ] Eduardo reviews attribution and decision-facing recommendations.
- [ ] Jose reviews architecture, transformation, and implementation recommendations.
- [ ] Client names, credentials, and production data are absent from reusable templates.

## Handoff

- [ ] Deliver all promised files in the agreed client-owned location.
- [ ] Present and record the findings session with permission.
- [ ] Capture one consolidated clarification round.
- [ ] Remove access that is no longer needed.
- [ ] Confirm whether the client will implement internally, use another team, or request a Prime proposal.
- [ ] Record actual hours by activity for pricing review.
